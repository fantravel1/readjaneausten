Northanger Abbey

Characters from Austen's Playful Gothic Parody (1817)

Northanger Abbey, Austen's earliest completed novel published posthumously, follows the naive Catherine Morland as she navigates Bath society and learns to distinguish between Gothic fantasy and reality. The cast includes witty friends, false companions, and mercenary guardians.

Main Characters

Protagonist

Catherine Morland

A naive seventeen-year-old whose love of Gothic novels leads her into comical misunderstandings. Her journey from innocence to experience forms the novel's core.

Naive Imaginative Good-hearted
Hero

Henry Tilney

A witty, intelligent clergyman who teases Catherine gently while genuinely caring for her. His ironic humor provides much of the novel's delight.

Witty Intelligent Kind
False Friend

Isabella Thorpe

A shallow, mercenary young woman who befriends Catherine while pursuing her brother James for his supposed fortune. Her fickleness is quickly revealed.

Shallow Mercenary False
Antagonist

John Thorpe

Isabella's boorish brother, a braggart and liar whose exaggeration of Catherine's wealth leads to General Tilney's mercenary interest in her.
