Sense and Sensibility

Characters from Austen's First Published Novel (1811)

Sense and Sensibility explores the contrasting temperaments of the Dashwood sisters through a rich cast of characters. From the rational Elinor to the passionate Marianne, and from the steady Colonel Brandon to the dashing but unreliable Willoughby, these characters embody the eternal tension between head and heart.

The Dashwood Sisters & Their Suitors

Protagonist

Elinor Dashwood

The eldest Dashwood sister, whose "sense" governs her conduct. She conceals her pain over Edward Ferrars behind a composed exterior, serving as the family's emotional anchor.

Rational Composed Self-controlled
Protagonist

Marianne Dashwood

The passionate middle sister whose "sensibility" leads her to love and heartbreak with equal intensity. Her romantic idealism is tested by Willoughby's betrayal.

Passionate Romantic Expressive
Hero

Edward Ferrars

A quiet, unassuming gentleman bound by a youthful secret engagement to Lucy Steele. His honor and constancy are tested as his feelings for Elinor deepen.

Honorable Reserved Constant
Hero

Colonel Brandon

A dignified man of thirty-five whose quiet devotion to Marianne is initially overlooked. His tragic past has deepened rather than hardened him.

Steadfast Honorable Devoted
Antagonist

John Willoughby

A handsome, charming young man who captures Marianne's heart before cruelly abandoning her. His attractive surface conceals moral weakness.

Charming Selfish Weak
Antagonist

Lucy Steele

A cunning young woman secretly engaged to Edward Ferrars. Her seeming friendship with Elinor masks jealousy and calculation.

Scheming Jealous Social-climbing

Family

Mrs. Dashwood

The emotional, romantic mother of Elinor, Marianne, and Margaret. Her sensibility mirrors Marianne's, making Elinor the family's practical anchor.
