Lucy Steele

The Scheming Rival

Character Overview

Lucy Steele is introduced as a young woman "naturally clever" whose lack of education has not been "corrected by the world." She is pretty, ingratiating, and thoroughly calculating‚Äîa social climber who uses every tool at her disposal.

Her secret engagement to Edward Ferrars gives her power, and she uses it ruthlessly. Her revelation to Elinor is calculated cruelty disguised as confidence-sharing, designed to establish her claim and wound her rival simultaneously.

Lucy represents a kind of female antagonist Austen returns to repeatedly: the woman whose apparent friendship masks jealousy and competition. Her sweet manners conceal a sharp mind devoted entirely to self-advancement.

The Social Climber

Lucy's goal throughout the novel is simple: to secure the best possible marriage. Her engagement to Edward was a gamble on his future prospects; when those prospects dim after his disinheritance, she quickly recalculates.

Her elopement with Robert Ferrars, Edward's younger brother who inherits everything, reveals her priorities clearly. She never loved Edward; she loved his position. When Robert becomes the better prospect, she transfers her affections without apparent difficulty.

The irony is that Lucy ultimately gets what she wants‚Äîwealth, position, the Ferrars inheritance‚Äîwhile Edward gets what he wants: Elinor and modest happiness. Lucy's success is hollow, achieved through manipulation and loveless marriage.
