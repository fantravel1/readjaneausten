Frank Churchill

The Charming Deceiver

Character Overview

Frank Churchill is the long-awaited son of Mr. Weston, raised by wealthy relatives and bearing their name. His eventual arrival in Highbury causes great excitement, and his charm lives up to expectations‚Äîperhaps too well.

Frank's defining characteristic is his ability to please. He says what people want to hear, adapts to every situation, and manages to make everyone like him. This facility, which seems so amiable, conceals a willingness to deceive that has serious consequences.

His secret engagement to Jane Fairfax drives much of the novel's plot. To conceal it, he flirts with Emma, encourages Highbury's speculation about Jane, and uses everyone around him as cover. His charm is real but so is his selfishness.

Charm and Deception

Frank's flirtation with Emma is conscious manipulation. He knows he is engaged; he uses Emma to deflect suspicion from his real attachment. That Emma enjoys his attention does not excuse his willingness to mislead her and damage her reputation.

His treatment of Jane is worse. To maintain secrecy, he pretends indifference to her in public, allows others to speculate about her coldness, and causes her genuine suffering. Love does not excuse the pain he inflicts through his deceptions.

Mr. Knightley's distrust of Frank is vindicated. Where others see charm, Knightley sees a fundamental unseriousness, a person who says whatever serves his immediate purpose. Frank is not evil, but he is not to be trusted.
