George Knightley

The Gentleman Who Speaks the Truth

Character Overview

George Knightley is thirty-seven years old, the owner of Donwell Abbey, and Emma's neighbor, brother-in-law (his brother married Emma's sister), and oldest friend. He has known her since childhood and is the only person who tells her uncomfortable truths.

Where others flatter Emma or defer to her, Knightley challenges her. He criticizes her matchmaking, defends Robert Martin against her snobbery, and ultimately delivers the rebuke after Box Hill that forces her moral awakening. His love is expressed through honest engagement, not flattery.

Knightley represents Austen's ideal of the English gentleman: responsible to his tenants, honest in his dealings, engaged with his community, and morally serious without being pompous. He is not perfect‚Äîhis jealousy of Frank Churchill is evident‚Äîbut he is consistently good.

Love Through Challenge

Knightley's love for Emma is evident from the beginning, though neither fully recognizes it until late in the novel. His concern for her character, his frustration at her mistakes, his jealousy of Frank Churchill‚Äîall signal deeper feeling than mere friendship.

What makes their relationship unique among Austen's couples is its long history and intellectual equality. Knightley does not need to discover Emma's worth; he has always known it. His task is to help her become the woman she can be, and hers is to recognize what has always been before her.

His willingness to move into Hartfield rather than take Emma away from her father shows his character. He sacrifices his own preference‚Äîto bring Emma to Donwell‚Äîbecause he understands what she owes to her father. True love, Austen suggests, accommodates the beloved's obligations.
