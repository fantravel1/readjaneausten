Character Encyclopedia

Every Character from Jane Austen's Novels

Jane Austen created some of the most memorable characters in English literature. From the witty Elizabeth Bennet to the steadfast Anne Elliot, her heroines and the rich cast of supporting characters continue to captivate readers more than two centuries later.

This encyclopedia provides detailed profiles of every significant character from Austen's six major novels, exploring their personalities, relationships, character arcs, and the themes they embody.

Austen's Character Types

The Spirited Heroine

Intelligent, witty, and morally grounded women who navigate society's constraints while maintaining their integrity and growing through experience.

Elizabeth Bennet, Emma Woodhouse, Catherine Morland

The Reformed Hero

Proud or reserved gentlemen whose genuine worth becomes apparent as they learn humility and demonstrate their true character.

Mr. Darcy, Mr. Knightley, Captain Wentworth

The Seductive Villain

Charming but morally bankrupt characters whose attractive exteriors conceal selfishness, dishonesty, or worse.

George Wickham, John Willoughby, Henry Crawford

The Comic Relief

Absurd, pompous, or foolish characters whose behavior provides humor while often serving as social satire.

Mr. Collins, Mrs. Bennet, Miss Bates

The Steady Suitor

Honorable men of quieter virtues who may be overlooked initially but prove their worth through constancy and integrity.

Colonel Brandon, Edward Ferrars, Edmund Bertram

The Sensible Sister

Calm, rational women who balance their more impetuous siblings or friends while managing their own quieter struggles.

Elinor Dashwood, Jane Bennet, Anne Elliot
