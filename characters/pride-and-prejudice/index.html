Pride and Prejudice

Characters from Austen's Most Beloved Novel (1813)

Pride and Prejudice features one of literature's most memorable casts of characters, from the spirited Elizabeth Bennet and proud Mr. Darcy to the wonderfully absurd Mr. Collins and the scheming Mr. Wickham. Together, they create a rich tapestry of Regency society.

Main Characters

Protagonist

Elizabeth Bennet

The second eldest Bennet daughter, known for her wit, intelligence, and strong sense of self. Her journey from prejudice to love forms the heart of the novel.

Witty Independent Perceptive
Hero

Fitzwilliam Darcy

A wealthy gentleman whose initial pride and aloofness mask a deeply honorable character. His transformation demonstrates the power of self-reflection.

Proud Honorable Reserved
Supporting

Jane Bennet

The eldest and most beautiful Bennet sister, whose gentle nature and genuine goodness sometimes blind her to others' faults.

Kind Beautiful Trusting
Supporting

Charles Bingley

Darcy's wealthy and amiable friend whose good nature is matched only by his susceptibility to the influence of others.

Amiable Wealthy Good-natured
Antagonist

George Wickham

A charming but deceitful militia officer whose handsome appearance conceals a history of dishonor and manipulation.

Charming Deceitful Self-serving
Antagonist

Lady Catherine de Bourgh

Darcy's formidable aunt, whose overbearing pride and sense of entitlement lead her to meddle disastrously in others' affairs.

Imperious Proud Meddling

The Bennet Family & Others

Family

Mr. Bennet

The witty but detached patriarch of the Bennet family, who retreats into his library to escape his wife's nerves and foolish daughters.

Witty Sardonic Detached
Family

Mrs. Bennet

The anxious, foolish mother whose single mission in life is to see her five daughters married, preferably to wealthy men.

Anxious Foolish Social-climbing
Family

Lydia Bennet

The youngest and wildest Bennet sister, whose thoughtless pursuit of officers leads to scandal and nearly ruins the family.

Flirtatious Thoughtless Wild
Comic Relief

Mr. Collins

The obsequious clergyman and heir to Longbourn, whose pompous speeches and fawning over Lady Catherine provide endless comedy.

Pompous Obsequious Absurd
Supporting

Charlotte Lucas

Elizabeth's sensible friend whose pragmatic choice to marry Mr. Collins highlights the limited options available to women of her time.

Practical Sensible Pragmatic
Supporting

Caroline Bingley

Bingley's snobbish sister who pursues Darcy relentlessly while looking down on the Bennets' inferior social standing.

Snobbish Jealous Scheming
Family

Mary Bennet

The plain middle Bennet sister who compensates with pedantic moralizing and painful piano performances, providing comic relief throughout.

Pedantic Plain Earnest
Family

Kitty Bennet

The impressionable fourth sister who follows Lydia's lead until separation and better influences allow her character to improve.

Impressionable Emotional Redeemable
Supporting

Georgiana Darcy

Darcy's shy, accomplished younger sister whose near-scandal with Wickham reveals his true villainy and her brother's protective love.

Shy Accomplished Wealthy
Supporting

Colonel Fitzwilliam

Darcy's amiable cousin whose easy manners contrast his reserved relation, and whose indiscretion reveals Darcy's interference with Bingley.

Amiable Pragmatic Indiscreet
